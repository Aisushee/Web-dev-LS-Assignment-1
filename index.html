About

The Olympic games is the biggest sporting event in the world. It is a platform where people from all over the world, from 205 different nations, irrespective of anything that make them different, gather to celebrate this biggest festival of sports. This sporting extravaganza happens every 4 years in different cities across the globe with this year's to be held in Paris and is all set to begin in the July month end.

Our Motto

The Olympic motto in Latin reads “Citius, Altius, Fortius – Communiter” which means “Faster, Higher, Stronger – Together” in English. The Olympic motto recognises the unifying power of sport and the importance of solidarity as the games itself delivers to the world beautiful lessons of unity and solidarity among people.

Our Logo

The 5 interlaced rings in the Olympic logo, each of which stands for the 5 continents (Blue for Europe, Yellow for Asia, Black for Africa, Green for Australia and Red for America), indicates a ‘World United by Emotions’.

Over the years

The Olympic Games boasts a history dating back well over 2,000 years. The first Olympic Games were held every four years in honour of the god Zeus.The first Olympic Games of the modern era took place in Athens, in the country where the original Games took place in Antiquity, in April 1896. The Paris 1900 Olympic Games saw women compete for the first time. Now, the games is back to paris in 2024 and lets gear up to experience the olympic extravaganza yet again!

Paris 2024

The 2024 Summer Olympics, officially the Games of the XXXIII Olympiad and officially branded as Paris 2024, is scheduled to take place from 26 July (the date of the opening ceremony) to 11 August 2024 in Paris, France. The programme of the 2024 Summer Olympics will feature 329 events in 32 sports, including the 28 "core" Olympic sports contested in 2016 and 2020,[63] and four optional sports that were proposed by the Paris Organising Committee.
